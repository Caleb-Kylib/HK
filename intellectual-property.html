Intellectual Property in Kenya

Intellectual Property (IP) law in Kenya is managed by two main bodies: the Kenya Industrial Property Institute (KIPI) for industrial property (patents, trademarks, industrial designs) and the Kenya Copyright Board (KECOBO) for creative works.

In a rapidly evolving innovation landscape, IP is often a business's most valuable asset. Protecting it requires timely registration and vigilant enforcement against infringement. Whether you are an artist, a tech entrepreneur, or a corporate entity, our firm provides comprehensive services to secure your IP rights domestically and through international frameworks like the ARIPO and WIPO systems.

Trademark Protection

A trademark distinguishes your goods or services from competitors. Under the Trademarks Act, registration provides exclusive rights to use the mark and enables you to take legal action against 'passing off' and unauthorized usage, safeguarding your brand's reputation.
