Corporate Compliance in Kenya: Duties of Directors and Shareholders

Navigating the legal obligations and responsibilities under the Companies Act, 2015

The Companies Act, 2015 and subsequent amendments define the responsibilities of directors and shareholders in managing corporations in Kenya. Directors owe fiduciary dutiesâ€”acting in good faith, exercising care, and avoiding conflicts of interest.

Key Statutory Requirement

All companies in Kenya must file annual returns with the Registrar of Companies within 42 days after the company's annual general meeting (AGM). Failure to comply can result in penalties of up to KES 500,000 for the company and KES 50,000 for each director in default.

Companies must maintain proper registers, hold annual general meetings, and file annual returns with the Registrar of Companies. Non-compliance can lead to heavy penalties and deregistration.

Directors' Duties and Responsibilities

Directors are the backbone of corporate governance and have both statutory and fiduciary duties under Kenyan law:

Fiduciary Duties
  • Act in good faith in the company's best interests
  • Exercise powers for proper purposes
  • Avoid conflicts of interest
  • Declare personal interests in company transactions
Duty of Care and Skill
  • Exercise reasonable care, skill, diligence
  • Keep informed about company affairs
  • Attend board meetings regularly
  • Make informed decisions based on adequate information
Financial Responsibilities
  • Ensure proper accounting records
  • Prepare and file annual financial statements
  • Ensure solvency and going concern
  • Prevent fraudulent trading
Compliance Duties
  • Comply with company's constitution
  • File statutory returns on time
  • Maintain statutory registers
  • Ensure tax compliance

Shareholders' Rights and Obligations

Shareholders, as the owners of the company, have specific rights and responsibilities:

Key Shareholder Rights
  • Right to attend and vote at general meetings
  • Right to receive dividends when declared
  • Right to inspect company records
  • Right to transfer shares (subject to articles)
  • Right to sue for wrongful acts
  • Right to participate in surplus assets on winding up
Shareholder Obligations
  • Pay for shares as agreed
  • Comply with the company's constitution
  • Act in good faith towards the company
  • Exercise rights for proper purposes
  • Respect the company's separate legal personality
  • Not to oppress minority shareholders

Annual Compliance Requirements

To maintain good standing, companies must fulfill these key annual requirements:

1. Annual General Meeting (AGM)

Must be held within 15 months of the previous AGM, but at least once every calendar year with no more than 6 months between meetings. Private companies with a single shareholder are exempt from holding AGMs.

2. Annual Returns

Must be filed within 42 days after the AGM, containing information about the company's directors, shareholders, share capital, and registered office.

3. Financial Statements

Must be prepared, audited (unless exempt), and presented to shareholders at the AGM. Small companies may qualify for audit exemption if they meet certain criteria.

4. Tax Compliance

Includes filing annual corporate tax returns, PAYE returns, VAT returns (if registered), and other relevant tax obligations with the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA).

Corporate Governance Best Practices

A proactive compliance culture helps attract investors, reduces regulatory risks, and supports long-term growth. Law firms can assist in company secretarial services, contract review, and governance audits to ensure adherence to statutory obligations.

Board Composition

Ensure a balanced mix of executive and non-executive directors, with appropriate committees (audit, remuneration, nomination).

Risk Management

Implement robust risk management frameworks to identify, assess, and mitigate business risks.

Stakeholder Engagement

Maintain open communication with shareholders, employees, customers, and the community.

Important: Non-compliance with corporate governance requirements can result in severe consequences, including personal liability for directors, fines, and in extreme cases, disqualification from acting as a director.
