Corporate Law in Kenya

Corporate law in Kenya is primarily governed by the Companies Act, 2015, which revolutionized business registration and management. It emphasizes transparency, ease of doing business, and protection of minority shareholders.

Whether you are a startup or an established multinational, navigating the regulatory environment requires a deep understanding of compliance requirements, including annual returns, beneficial ownership disclosures, and corporate governance standards as set by the Business Registration Service (BRS).

Director and Shareholder Duties

Under Kenyan law, directors owe fiduciary duties to the company. They must act in good faith, exercise reasonable care, and avoid conflicts of interest. Shareholders' rights are bolstered by the Act, allowing for derivative actions and ensuring fair treatment in corporate decisions.
