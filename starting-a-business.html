Starting a Business in Kenya

A Legal Step-by-Step Guide for Entrepreneurs

Kenya offers a vibrant environment for business. However, success starts with proper legal foundation—from choosing the right entity to ensuring tax compliance.

1. Choosing your Entity

You can register as a Sole Proprietorship, Partnership, or a Limited Liability Company (LLC). Most modern businesses opt for an LLC due to its separate legal personality.

2. Company Registration

Registration is done through the e-Citizen portal. You will need a unique business name, articles of association, and details of directors and shareholders.

3. Post-Registration Compliance

  • KRA PIN for the company.
  • NHIF & NSSF registration for employees.
  • Unified Business Permit from the County Government.
  • Relevant industry-specific licenses.
