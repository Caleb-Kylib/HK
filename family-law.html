Family Law in Kenya

Family law in Kenya is primarily governed by the Marriage Act, 2014, the Children Act, 2022, and the Matrimonial Property Act, 2013. It covers a wide range of sensitive issues from marriage and divorce to child custody and succession.

Kenyan law recognizes various types of marriages: Christian, Hindu, Muslim, Customary, and Civil. Each has unique legal requirements for registration and dissolution. Modern reforms have focused on protecting the "best interests of the child" in all legal proceedings and ensuring equitable division of matrimonial property based on contribution.

Matrimonial Property & Custody

In cases of separation, the courts prioritize the welfare of children. Matrimonial property disputes now consider both financial and non-financial contributions (such as domestic work and child-rearing), ensuring a fairer transition for all parties involved.

Mediation First

The Kenyan legal system now encourages alternative dispute resolution (ADR) for family matters. Mediation can save families time, cost, and emotional distress compared to traditional litigation.
