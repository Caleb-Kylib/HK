Succession Laws in Kenya 2025: The Ultimate Practical Guide

Understanding your rights and responsibilities to prevent family disputes

Succession causes more family disputes in Kenya than any other area of law. Understanding the current rules will save your family years of costly litigation.

1. Which Law Applies to Your Estate in 2025?

Deceased Person Law that Applies Can Write a Will?
Christians, Hindus, others Law of Succession Act (Cap 160) – Part V (intestate) or Part III (testate) Yes – full freedom
Muslims Kadhi’s Court – Islamic law (Qur’an & Sunnah) for up to 1/3; remainder intestate under LSA Yes – limited to 1/3
Customary law Only applies if the deceased expressly opted out in writing or was governed by customary law before 1981 Very limited

2. How to Write a Valid Will in Kenya 2025

  • In writing
  • Signed by testator (or thumbprint + 2 witnesses if illiterate)
  • Two competent witnesses (18+, sound mind, not beneficiaries)
  • Age 18 and above

2025 Trends & Tips: Oral wills only valid for active armed forces. Digital/electronic wills NOT recognized unless printed, signed and witnessed. Video wills are evidence of capacity but not a substitute.

Recommended clauses in 2025:

  • Gift of digital assets (crypto wallets, KRA iTax logins, M-Pesa)
  • Appointment of a professional executor (advocate or bank)
  • Trust for minors or spendthrift beneficiaries
  • Residue clause to avoid partial intestacy

3. Intestate Succession (No Will) – 2025 Rules

Surviving Relatives Who Inherits What (in order)
Spouse + children Spouse: life interest OR personal effects + 20% outright; children share rest equally (per stirpes)
Spouse only (no children) Spouse takes everything
Children only (no spouse) Children share equally
No spouse, no children Parents → siblings → kindred → State (bona vacantia)

4. Dependency Claims – “Provision for Dependants” (Section 26–29)

Even if you are disinherited in a will, you can apply within 2 years of death for “reasonable provision” if you were:

  • Spouse or former spouse
  • Child (minor or adult unable to maintain themselves)
  • Dependent parent, sibling, niece/nephew

5. Step-by-Step Probate/Administration Process in 2025

A. With a Will (Grant of Probate)
  1. File petition at High Court (Family Division) or Magistrate’s Court (estates <KES 2M)
  2. Gazette notice (14 days) + Kenya Gazette online
  3. Obtain Grant (average 4–9 months in Nairobi, faster in Kadhi’s Courts)
  4. Pay estate duty (none) and transmit properties
B. Without a Will (Letters of Administration)

Same process but surviving spouse/children must agree on administrator or court appoints Public Trustee.

2025 e-Filing Update: 100% online filing via eFiling portal. Pay court fees via M-Pesa. Confirmation hearings now mostly virtual.

6. Taxes on Inheritance in 2025

  • No estate duty / inheritance tax
  • Capital Gains Tax: 15% final tax on transfer of land/shares (paid by executor)
  • Income tax on rental income continues

7. Common 2025 Succession Traps & How to Avoid Them

Trap Solution
Writing a will without witnesses Always use two disinterested witnesses
Leaving everything to one child Expect dependency claims from others
Dying before transmitting property from parents’ estate Leads to double succession – transmit immediately
Muslim writing a will for more than 1/3 Kadhi’s Court will strike down excess
Forgetting beneficial ownership disclosure on company shares Company cannot be transmitted until disclosed

8. Timeline Summary (Nairobi Registry 2025)

  • Death to filing petition: 30–180 days
  • Filing to grant: 4–12 months
  • Grant to full distribution: 6–18 months
