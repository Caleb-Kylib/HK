Compassionate and confidential legal guidance for personal and family matters.

Family Law in Kenya

Family law in Kenya is governed by the Marriage Act, 2014, the Children Act, 2022, and the Matrimonial Property Act, 2013 — covering marriage, divorce, custody, and succession.

Kenyan law recognizes Christian, Hindu, Muslim, Customary, and Civil marriages. Recent reforms focus on the best interests of the child and equitable property division based on each party's contribution.

Matrimonial Property & Custody

Courts prioritize child welfare in custody matters. Property disputes consider both financial and non-financial contributions, including domestic work and child-rearing, when determining equitable division.
