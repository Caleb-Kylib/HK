Kenya Immigration Guide 2025: How to Secure Visas, Work Permits, and Permanent Residency

Practical, up-to-date guidance following the 2024 e-Immigration overhaul and Kenya Citizenship & Immigration Regulations 2024.

Published: 24 November 2025   |   Author: Your Firm Name – Immigration Law Department

Kenya remains one of Africa’s top destinations for investors, professionals, retirees and digital nomads. After the 2024 overhaul introducing the e-Immigration system, this guide summarises the most current rules and practical steps for 2025–2026.

1. Do You Need a Visa to Enter Kenya in 2025?

  • Visa-free: Citizens of 51 countries (all EAC states, most Commonwealth countries, plus Germany, South Korea, etc.) — 90 days, renewable once.
  • eTA (Electronic Travel Authorisation): Required for all visa-exempt visitors from 1 January 2025. Fee: USD 30. Apply at least 72 hours before travel.
  • Visa-required nationalities: Apply online for an e-Visa — Single-entry USD 51; East Africa Tourist Visa USD 101.
Note: Always confirm country-specific rules before travel — eTA rules mean even visa-exempt travellers must obtain authorisation.

2. Main Classes of Work Permits in Kenya (2025 Fees & Requirements)

Class Who Qualifies Minimum Salary / Investment Duration 2025 Fee (KES)
Class A Investors / shareholders KES 15,000,000 guaranteed investment OR KES 100,000 monthly turnover 2 years (renewable) 400,000
Class B Directors (non-shareholder) Company must prove no Kenyan can do the job 2 years 300,000
Class C Skilled employees under contract Proven qualifications + Kenyan understudy plan 2 years 250,000
Class D Offered job no Kenyan can do Degree + 5 years experience 3 years 400,000

Class M Refugees & protected persons Recognised by RAS 2 years Free
2025 Changes: All applications are now 100% online via the eFNS portal (efns.immigration.go.ke). Processing times reduced to 21–45 days. The understudy requirement (training a Kenyan to take over within 3 years) is strictly enforced.

3. Special Passes and Short-Term Options

  • Special Pass: KES 15,000 per month — for applicants finalising work permit applications.
  • Dependants Pass: Spouse & children under 18 — KES 50,000 per year each.
  • Student Pass: For full-time registered institutions only.

4. Permanent Residency in Kenya (2025 Routes)

You may apply for indefinite leave to remain after legally living in Kenya for 7 continuous years on any permit (reduced from the previous 10-year requirement). Fast-track routes include:

Category Qualifying Period Key Requirement
Spouse of Kenyan citizen 3 years Valid marriage certificate + affidavit
Former Kenyan citizen Immediate Proof of previous citizenship
High-net-worth investor 2 years USD 500,000 invested + creation of 10 Kenyan jobs
Exceptional talent (Class P) Case-by-case Arts, sports, science, academia

2025 Fee: KES 1,500,000 (non-refundable application fee) + KES 500,000 payable on approval.

5. Step-by-Step Application Process (2025)

  1. Create and verify an account on the eFNS portal.
  2. Upload certified copies: passport, police clearance (<6 months), CV, academic certificates.
  3. Pay via M-Pesa, card, or bank transfer.
  4. Book a biometric appointment (available in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu, Eldoret).
  5. Track the application in real-time — SMS and email alerts are mandatory.
  6. Collect permit sticker at Nyayo House or regional immigration office.
Tip: Use the eFNS portal’s employer and sponsor pre-registration feature before submitting employee permit applications to avoid delays.

6. Common Rejection Reasons (and How to Avoid Them)

  • Incomplete Kenyan understudy plan (accounts for ~40% of rejections) — include clear milestones and training timelines.
  • Police clearance older than 6 months — obtain a fresh certificate before upload.
  • Company not pre-registered on the eFNS employer portal — register early.
  • Overstaying previous visa/permit — automatic 5-year re-entry ban applies.
  • Using unregistered agents — only advocates of the High Court may lawfully represent applicants.

7. Penalties for Immigration Offences (2025)

  • Overstaying: KES 5,000 per day (max KES 500,000) or up to 3 years imprisonment.
  • Working on wrong permit: KES 1,000,000 fine + deportation + 5-year re-entry ban.
  • Employer hiring without permit: KES 2,000,000 per employee.

8. Citizenship by Registration (2025)

Eligibility after 7 years of lawful residence plus permanent residency status. Dual citizenship is now fully permitted for adults. Processing typically takes 6–12 months.
