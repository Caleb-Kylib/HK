Immigration Guide to Kenya

Navigating work permits, residency, and citizenship

Kenya is a hub for international business and tourism. Navigating the immigration landscape requires a clear understanding of the various classes of work permits and residency requirements.

Work Permit Classes

  • Class A: For persons entering prospecting for minerals or mining.
  • Class B: For agriculture and animal husbandry.
  • Class D: Employment permits for professionals.
  • Class G: For specific trade, business, or consultancy.

Permanent Residency

Expatriates who have held work permits for at least seven years and have resided in Kenya for at least three years may apply for Permanent Residency.
