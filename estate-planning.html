Succession Laws in Kenya 2025: The Ultimate Practical Guide

Protecting your legacy and ensuring a smooth transition of wealth across generations.

Jul 15, 2025 Grace Njoroge

Quick Answer

Estate planning in Kenya is governed by the Law of Succession Act (Cap 160). A valid Will, properly witnessed, is the most effective way to protect your family from lengthy probate disputes and ensure your assets are distributed according to your wishes.

Estate planning in Kenya is primarily governed by the Law of Succession Act (Cap 160). This Act provides the legal framework for the distribution of a person's property after their death, whether they left a Will (testate) or not (intestate).

Modern estate planning goes beyond just writing a Will. It involves the creation of Trusts, drafting of Power of Attorneys, and strategic tax considerations to minimize the burden on beneficiaries. Our firm provides a sensitive and structured approach to help you organize your affairs, ensuring that your wishes are respected and your loved ones are provided for without legal complications.

The Importance of a Valid Will

A Will must be properly executed — signed by the testator in the presence of two witnesses who are not beneficiaries — to be legally binding. In the absence of a Will, the Act provides a strict formula for distribution that may not align with your specific wishes, often leading to family disputes.
