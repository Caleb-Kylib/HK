Modern Survey Equipment Explained

Updated: January 10, 2026 Tech Team 6 Min Read

Quick Answer

Modern surveying utilizes three core technologies: 1. GNSS (GPS) for satellite-based coordinate fixing; 2. Drones for rapid, high-resolution aerial mapping of large parcels; and 3. Robotic Total Stations for high-precision optical measurement in urban construction sites.

In This Article:
  • GNSS: Precision from Space
  • Drones & Aerial Photogrammetry
  • Robotic Total Stations
  • Why Tech Investment Matters for Clients

Surveying has evolved drastically from the era of heavy chains and hand-drawn sketches. At GeoAxis, we leverage the latest technology to ensure your boundaries are accurate within millimeters.

1. GNSS: Precision from Space

GNSS (Global Navigation Satellite System) receivers like the ones we use are vastly superior to standard phone GPS. By connecting to multiple constellations (GPS, GLONASS, Galileo), we can lock onto a position anywhere in Kenya with extreme repeatability.

2. Drones & Aerial Photogrammetry

Mapping thousands of acres of difficult terrain no longer takes weeks. Our mapping drones capture thousands of overlapping photos, which our software then stitches into a single, geo-referenced orthomosaic map. This is perfect for topographic surveys over large agricultural lands.

Experience High-Tech Precision

3. Robotic Total Stations

In dense urban environments like Nairobi, satellite signals are often blocked by skyscrapers. Here, we use robotic total stations—advanced lasers that measure distance and angles by reflecting off of prisms. They are essential for setting out building foundations precisely.

4. Why Tech Investment Matters for Clients

Investing in this equipment allows us to work faster, reduce human error, and provide you with digital files compatible with the latest CAD and GIS software. This ensures your data is ready for architects and engineers immediately.
