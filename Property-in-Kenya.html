Buying Off-Plan Property & Tax Disputes in Kenya (2025)

A comprehensive guide on property buyer protections, escrow accounts, sectional titles & tax objection procedures

Buying Off-Plan Property in Kenya 2025

Buying property off‑plan remains a preferred investment option in Kenya despite risks from previous developer failures. Recent legal reforms now provide stronger buyer protections.

1. What is an Off‑Plan Purchase?

You purchase a home based on architectural plans before completion. Ownership transfers only after completion and issuance of a sectional title.

2. Key Laws Regulating Off‑Plan Purchases

  • Sectional Properties Act 2020
  • Sectional Properties Regulations 2021
  • Consumer Protection Act 2012
  • Land Registration Act 2012
  • Immigration Regulations (foreign buyers)
Mandatory Escrow Accounts

Developers cannot legally receive payments directly. Funds must be deposited into a regulated escrow account jointly managed by a bank, developer, and independent advocate or manager.

3. Escrow Account Requirements

  • Held in a licensed Kenyan bank
  • Account name must include "Escrow" and project name
  • Funds released only in construction stages
  • Penalties for breach: up to KES 5M or 5‑year imprisonment

4. Sectional Titles

A buyer only receives a sectional title once the main title is surrendered, plans approved, and an Occupation Certificate issued.

Expected timeline: 6–18 months after practical completion.

5. Documents You Must Request

  • Approved sectional plan
  • Mother title + official search
  • Escrow agreement + bank confirmation
  • NEMA license and approved building plans
  • NCA registration & professional indemnity

6. Red Flags to Avoid

  • Payments made outside escrow
  • Promises of 6‑month title issuance
  • Sale agreements waiving refund rights
  • Lack of a sinking fund clause

7. Buyer Rights if Developer Defaults

  • Refund plus 15% annual interest
  • Right to petition court for project administration
  • Escrow funds cannot settle developer debts

Tax Disputes & KRA Objections in Kenya (2025)

The law provides a clear procedure for objecting to tax assessments from the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA).

1. Types of Assessments You Can Challenge

  • Additional income tax, VAT, excise
  • Default and amended assessments
  • Penalty and interest notices
  • Objectionable decisions
30‑Day Deadline: You must file an objection within 30 days, or the assessment becomes final.

2. How to File a Valid Objection

  1. Login to iTax & complete the Objection Form
  2. State specific grounds
  3. Attach supporting documents
  4. Pay or secure 50% of disputed tax
  5. Serve objection through iTax or physically

3. If KRA Rejects or Partially Allows Your Objection

You have 30 days to appeal to the Tax Appeals Tribunal (TAT). Filing stays enforcement of disputed tax.

4. Remission of Penalties & Interest

You may seek remission based on reasonable cause, first‑time compliance or hardship.

5. Execution Limits During Dispute

  • No agency appointments without court/TAT order
  • No property auction where undisputed tax is paid
  • Right to apply for stay of execution
