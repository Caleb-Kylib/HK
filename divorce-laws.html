Divorce Laws & Procedures in Kenya

Navigating sensitive family matters with legal expertise and empathy

Divorce in Kenya is primarily governed by the Marriage Act, 2014. The grounds for divorce vary depending on the type of marriage (Civil, Christian, Muslim, Hindu, or Customary).

Grounds for Divorce

  • Adultery.
  • Cruelty (mental or physical).
  • Desertion for a period of at least three years.
  • Exceptional hardship or depravity.
  • Irretrievable breakdown of the marriage.

Child Custody & Maintenance

The court's primary consideration is the "best interests of the child." Both parents have a legal obligation to provide for the child's welfare, education, and health.
