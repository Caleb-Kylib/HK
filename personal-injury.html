Personal Injury Law in Kenya

Personal injury law in Kenya is based on the Law of Torts and specific statutes like the Occupiers' Liability Act and the Work Injury Benefits Act (WIBA). It allows individuals who have suffered harm due to the negligence of others to seek financial compensation (damages).

Proving liability requires showing that a duty of care was owed, that this duty was breached, and that the breach directly caused the injury. Whether it's a road traffic accident, medical malpractice, or a workplace injury, our firm is dedicated to ensuring that victims receive the maximum compensation possible for medical expenses, loss of income, and pain and suffering.

WIBA Claims

Under the Work Injury Benefits Act, employers are required to provide insurance for their employees against injuries or diseases contracted in the course of employment. WIBA provides a structured compensation scale, and we help employees navigate the process of filing claims with the Director of Occupational Safety and Health Services.
