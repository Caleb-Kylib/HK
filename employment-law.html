Employment Law in Kenya

Employment law in Kenya is heavily regulated by the Employment Act, 2007, the Labour Relations Act, 2007, and the Labour Institutions Act, 2007. These laws provide a framework for fair labour practices, minimum standards of service, and the resolution of workplace conflicts.

The Employment and Labour Relations Court (ELRC) has exclusive jurisdiction over all labour-related matters. Recent legal trends involve strict enforcement of fair termination procedures and the protection of employee data privacy. Whether drafting complex employment contracts or handling sensitive redundancy processes, our firm ensures that the law is followed to the letter.

Unfair Dismissal & Procedure

Under Kenyan law, a termination must be both substantively justified and procedurally fair. Employers must provide a valid reason for termination and follow a process that includes a disciplinary hearing and the right to be accompanied by a trade union representative or colleague.
