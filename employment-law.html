Employment Law Updates 2025: Rights and Responsibilities in the Workplace

Key changes and compliance requirements under Kenya's updated labor laws

Kenya's Employment Act, 2007, together with its amendments, provides comprehensive protection for employees and defines employer duties. The 2025 reforms emphasize fair termination procedures, inclusive hiring, and the mandatory housing and social security deductions.

Important Update (2025)

The Employment (Amendment) Act, 2025 introduces significant changes to workplace regulations, including extended parental leave, enhanced protections for gig workers, and stricter penalties for workplace discrimination.

Employers must review contracts and HR policies to remain compliant, while employees should understand their rights regarding leave, working hours, and termination.

Key Changes in the 2025 Employment Law Reforms

Enhanced Parental Leave

  • Paternity leave increased from 2 to 4 weeks
  • Adoption leave now 12 weeks (up from 3 months)
  • New 10-day paid family responsibility leave

Housing Levy

  • 1.5% housing levy on gross salary (employee)
  • Matching 1.5% contribution from employer
  • Funds accessible for affordable housing

Gig Worker Protections

  • Platform workers now entitled to minimum wage
  • Right to form/join unions
  • Access to NSSF & NHIF benefits

Social Security Updates

  • Increased NSSF contribution rates
  • NHIF restructuring with income-based bands
  • Enhanced maternity benefits

Termination and Redundancy: New Requirements

The 2025 amendments introduce stricter regulations around employment termination:

Key Change: Mandatory Pre-Termination Meeting

Employers must now conduct a pre-termination meeting with the employee before issuing any termination notice, except in cases of gross misconduct. The employee has the right to be accompanied by a colleague or union representative.

Fair Reasons for Termination

Termination must be based on one of the following fair reasons:

Conduct

Misconduct or poor performance after proper warnings and opportunities to improve.

Redundancy

Genuine redundancy situations with proper consultation and selection process.

Capability

Lack of qualifications or capability to perform the job.

Legal Requirements

Where continued employment would violate the law.

Workplace Discrimination and Harassment

The 2025 amendments expand protections against workplace discrimination and harassment:

Protected Characteristic Examples of Prohibited Conduct Employer Responsibilities
Gender & Pregnancy Pregnancy discrimination, sexual harassment, unequal pay Equal pay audits, anti-harassment policies, training
Disability Failure to provide reasonable accommodations Workplace adjustments, accessibility improvements
Age Age-based hiring/firing, mandatory retirement ages Objective hiring criteria, flexible work options
HIV Status Mandatory testing, termination due to status Confidentiality, anti-stigma training

Remote Work Regulations

With the rise of remote work, the 2025 amendments introduce specific provisions:

Remote Work Policy Requirements

Employers must have a written remote work policy addressing:

  • Eligibility criteria for remote work
  • Work hours and availability expectations
  • Equipment and expense reimbursement
  • Data security and confidentiality
  • Workplace health and safety in home offices

Compliance Checklist for Employers

To ensure compliance with the 2025 employment law changes, employers should:

Policy Updates
  • Review and update employment contracts
  • Develop remote work policies
  • Update anti-discrimination and harassment policies
  • Create mental health and wellness policies
Staff Training
  • Conduct diversity and inclusion training
  • Train managers on new termination procedures
  • Provide mental health awareness training
  • Educate on data protection requirements
Payroll Updates
  • Implement new housing levy deductions
  • Update NSSF and NHIF contributions
  • Review salary structures for pay equity
  • Document overtime and leave calculations
Compliance
  • Conduct equal pay audits
  • Update health and safety assessments
  • Review recruitment practices
  • Document disciplinary procedures
Note: The information provided in this article is for general informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Employment law is complex and constantly evolving. For specific legal advice regarding your situation, please consult with a qualified employment law attorney.
