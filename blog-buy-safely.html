How to Buy Land Safely in Kenya: A Complete Checklist

Stop land fraud before it starts — a comprehensive 2026 checklist for secure property purchases.

Feb 20, 2026 Legal Team

Quick Answer

Buying land safely requires four non-negotiable steps: 1. Physical site visit to confirm existence; 2. Official registry search on ArdhiSasa; 3. Beacon verification by a licensed surveyor; and 4. Using a lawyer for the sale agreement. Skipping any step increases fraud risk significantly.

Buying land in Kenya is an excellent investment, but it is often plagued by fraudulent titles and unofficial agents. To ensure your hard-earned money is safe, follow this expert legal guide.

1. Why the Site Visit is Mandatory

Never buy land that you haven't stepped on. A site visit allows you to see the terrain, check for encroachments (like neighbors' fences or road reserves), and talk to people in the area who might know the true history of the land.

2. Conducting an Official Search Correctly

With the digitization of the Ministry of Lands, most searches are now performed on the ArdhiSasa portal. A search reveals the registered owner, size, and any encumbrances like bank loans or court cases.

3. The Role of Licensed Surveyors

Just because a seller shows you beacons doesn't mean they are in the right place. A licensed surveyor will cross-reference the physical beacons with official maps using high-precision equipment.

4. Finalizing the Sale Agreement

Once satisfied with the search and survey, engage an advocate to draft the sale agreement. Payments should be made through a lawyer's account to ensure a legal trail of the transaction.
