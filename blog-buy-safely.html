How to Buy Land Safely in Kenya: A Complete Checklist

Updated: February 20, 2026 Mercy Achieng 12 Min Read

Quick Answer

Buying land safely requires four non-negotiable steps: 1. Physical site visit to confirm the land exists; 2. Official registry search on ArdhiSasa; 3. Beacon verification by a licensed surveyor; and 4. Using a lawyer for the sale agreement. Skipping any of these increases your fraud risk by 90%.

In This Article:
  • 1. Why the Site Visit is Mandatory
  • 2. Conducting an Official Search Correctly
  • 3. The Role of Licensed Surveyors
  • 4. Finalizing the Sale Agreement

Buying land in Kenya is an excellent investment, but it is often plagued by "briefcase" agents and fraudulent titles. To ensure your hard-earned money is safe, follow this expert guide.

1. Why the Site Visit is Mandatory

Never buy land that you haven't stepped on. A site visit allows you to see the terrain, check for encroachments (like neighbors' fences or road reserves), and talk to people in the area who might know the true history of the land.

2. Conducting an Official Search Correctly

With the digitization of the Ministry of Lands, most searches are now performed on the ArdhiSasa portal. A search reveals the registered owner, the size of the land, and any encumbrances like bank loans or court cases.

Don't Risk Your Savings

Our surveyors can perform a "Registry vs. Ground" verification to ensure the property on paper actually exists on the map.

Get Professional Verification

3. The Role of Licensed Surveyors

Just because a seller shows you beacons doesn't mean they are in the right place. A licensed surveyor will cross-reference the physical beacons with the official Survey of Kenya maps using high-precision GNSS equipment.

Learn more about why this is essential in our guide to Beacon Verification.

4. Finalizing the Sale Agreement

Once you are satisfied with the search and the survey, engage a lawyer to draft the sale agreement. Payments should ideally be made through a lawyer's account to ensure there is a legal trail of the transaction.
