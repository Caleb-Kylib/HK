Criminal Defense

Upholding the presumption of innocence and protecting your constitutional rights.

Criminal Law in Kenya

Criminal law in Kenya is primarily governed by the Penal Code (Cap 63) and the Criminal Procedure Code (Cap 75). Article 50 of the Constitution of Kenya guarantees every accused person the right to a fair trial, which includes the right to be represented by an advocate.

Facing criminal charges is a life-altering experience. Our firm provides a rigorous and strategic defense for a wide range of offenses, from white-collar crimes and economic offenses to traffic violations and serious felonies. We focus on ensuring that law enforcement agencies adhere to the due process of law, from the point of arrest through to the final judgment.

Constitutional Rights

Under the Bill of Rights, you have the right to remain silent, the right to be informed of the reason for your arrest, and the right to be brought before a court within 24 hours. We act swiftly to secure bail and bond and to challenge any violations of your fundamental rights.
