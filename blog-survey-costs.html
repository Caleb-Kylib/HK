Cost of Land Survey in Kenya: 2026 Guide

March 11, 2026 Legal Team

Quick Answer

A simple boundary check in Kenya typically ranges from KES 15,000 to KES 30,000. Complex subdivisions and larger topographic mapping can range from KES 50,000 to over KES 200,000 depending on the location and terrain.

Factors Influencing Survey Costs

The cost of surveying is not arbitrary but depends on several professional factors:

  • Location: Mobilization to remote areas increases costs.
  • Type of Survey: Boundary checks are cheaper than topographic mapping.
  • Terrain: Difficult terrain takes more time and precision.
  • Legal Documentation: Filing fees at the Ministry of Lands are separate from professional labor.

Hidden Costs to Budget For

Don't forget to account for:

  • Land Search Fees: KES 500 per search via ArdhiSasa.
  • Beacon Materials: Concrete beacons for boundary marking.
  • Registry Filing: Government stamp duty and registration fees.
