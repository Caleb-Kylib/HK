How Much Does a Land Survey Cost in Kenya? [2026 Price Guide]

Updated: March 11, 2026 Surv. Martin Kimani 10 Min Read

Quick Answer

The cost of land survey in Kenya typically starts from KES 15,000 to KES 30,000 for a simple boundary check on a standard residential plot. However, complex subdivisions and topographic mapping for large acreage can range from KES 50,000 to over KES 200,000 depending on location, terrain, and the required legal documentation.

  • 1. Understanding LSBN Statutory Fees
  • 2. Top 5 Factors Influencing Survey Costs
  • 3. Hidden Costs You Should Budget For

Navigating the financial aspect of land ownership in Kenya often leads to one critical question: "How much will the surveyor charge?" While pricing varies, it is not arbitrary. Fees are guided by the Land Surveyors Board of Kenya (LSBN) to ensure professional standards and prevent price gouging.

1. Understanding LSBN Statutory Fees

The Land Surveyors Board of Kenya sets minimum scale of fees. These are the "base" prices for different professional tasks. For instance, the professional fee for a simple mutation (subdivision) starts from a set minimum, plus a per-plot fee for every additional parcel created.

2. Top 5 Factors Influencing Survey Costs

  • A. Location (Nairobi vs. Rural): Transport and mobilization to remote areas in Narok or Turkana will increase the final quote.
  • B. Type of Survey: A Boundary Survey is generally cheaper than a comprehensive Topographic Survey needed for engineering designs.
  • C. Terrain Difficulty: Hilly, swampy, or heavily forested land takes more field hours to map compared to flat, open ground.
  • D. Legal Paperwork: Filing fees for mutation forms at the Ministry of Lands and search fees are fixed and separate from professional labor.
  • E. Urgency: Accelerated projects requiring overtime field visits or "fast-track" processing may attract a premium.

3. Hidden Costs You Should Budget For

When planning your budget, don't just look at the surveyor's quote. You must also account for:

  • Land Search Fees: KES 500 per search via ArdhiSasa.
  • Beacon Material: The cost of the concrete beacons themselves (if not included in the quote).
  • Registry Filing: Government stamp duty and registration fees for new titles.

4. How to Save on Your Land Survey

The best way to save money is to do it right the first time. Hiring an unlicensed person ("quack") might seem cheaper, but if the Ministry of Lands rejects your survey, you will pay twice. Always internal link to our guide on Beacon Verification to ensure your purchase is secure.

Additionally, if you are a developer, subdividing multiple plots at once reduces the "per-plot" cost significantly compared to doing them individually over several years.
